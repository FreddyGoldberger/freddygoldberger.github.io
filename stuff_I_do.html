SmartCurtain Prototype 4

The SmartCurtain, is a low cost module designed to automate the opening and closing of blinds, providing convenience and confort for users. By leveraging the power of the ESP32 WiFi connectivity. The end goal of the SmartCurtain is to offer seamless control and integration into your smart home ecosystem.

At the heart of SmartCurtain lies a PID control algorithm. This algorithm enables precise and smooth movement of the blinds by utilizing a low speed, high torque DC motor equipped with an encoder. The PID control algorithm ensures accurate positioning and guaranteeing a reliable and consistent user experience.

SmartCurtain's WiFi compatibility allows users to effortlessly operate the module from anywhere within their network. Whether you're at home or away, you can conveniently control the blinds' movements through a dedicated mobile app or integrate it with your existing smart home automation system. This level of control brings enhanced convenience, security, and energy efficiency to your living space.

WiMega3

The WiMega3, was designed to enhance the wireless control capabilities of commercially available relay boards. By integrating the WiMega3 module with a relay board, users gain the ability to remotely manage the ON/OFF state of the relays through wireless connectivity. This module leverages the nRF24L01+ wireless communication module, facilitating seamless and reliable communication between the relays and the control interface.

The WiMega3 module interfaces with the relay board via the WiMegaUSB connector, which establishes connectivity with a computer. This connection allows users to access a user-friendly graphical user interface (GUI) application, enabling intuitive control over the state of the devices connected to the relays. Through the GUI application, users can conveniently toggle the relays ON or OFF with just a few clicks, eliminating the need for manual switching or physical access to the relay board.

This project offers a flexible and convenient solution for remotely controlling various devices connected to the relays, streamlining operations and enhancing user experience. With WiMega3, managing the power state of connected devices becomes effortless, providing users with greater control and convenience over their systems.

WiMega1

The "WiMega" was an ambitious project consisting of a module designed to enhance the communication capabilities of the ATMega328 microcontroller. Developed during a time when alternative technologies for IoT were either expensive or nonexistent, WiMega provided an innovative solution for low-power wireless communication.

The primary objective of WiMega was to empower the ATMega328 microcontroller with the ability to communicate wirelessly with other modules. This was achieved by integrating the nRF24L01+ module, known for its efficient low-power communication, into the design.

During the development of WiMega, the focus was on creating an affordable and accessible IoT module. With limited options available at the time.

Tiny Irrigation Controller

Introducing "Tiny Irrigation," a simple project developed for System Coalition, a non-profit organization dedicated to making science and engineering enjoyable and accessible for children. The Tiny Irrigation module serves as an interactive tool that engages young minds in understanding the principles of automation and plant care.

This module consists of an electric pump, a humidity sensor, and is programmable using the Arduino IDE, a popular platform for coding microcontrollers. The objective of the project is to empower children to create their own code that enables the module to pump water periodically to a plant when the soil moisture level is low.

By utilizing the Arduino programming language, young participants can develop algorithms that monitor the humidity sensor's readings. When the soil moisture falls below a certain threshold, the module activates the electric pump to provide water to the plant. This interactive experience not only fosters an understanding of coding concepts but also promotes a hands-on approach to nurturing plants and understanding the importance of proper irrigation.

Through the Tiny Irrigation project, System Coalition aims to inspire curiosity, problem-solving skills, and creativity in children while instilling an appreciation for science and engineering. By combining real-world applications with coding, young participants can actively contribute to the well-being of plants and explore the fascinating intersection of technology and nature.

With the Tiny Irrigation module, children are encouraged to explore the possibilities of automation, environmental monitoring, and sustainable practices in a fun and engaging manner. It paves the way for the next generation of innovators, empowering them with practical knowledge and a passion for creating a positive impact on the world around them.
